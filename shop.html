Panjabi

The panjabi is a graceful and elegant long tunic that flows effortlessly down to the knees. Made from soft, breathable fabrics like cotton or silk, it often features intricate embroidery or delicate patterns that add a touch of sophistication. The panjabi is a symbol of cultural pride and is worn with a sense of dignity and tradition.

Sherwani

The sherwani is a regal, knee-length coat that exudes grandeur and elegance. Crafted from luxurious fabrics such as brocade, silk, or velvet, it is often adorned with elaborate embroidery, sequins, and beads. The sherwani is typically worn over a kurta and churidar, making it a popular choice for weddings and formal occasions. Its majestic appearance reflects the rich heritage and royal history of Bangladesh.

Pagri

The pagri, or turban, is a traditional headwear that signifies honor and respect. Made from a long piece of cloth, it is skillfully wrapped around the head in a variety of styles. The pagri can be plain or decorated with intricate designs, and it often complements the wearer's attire. It is a symbol of cultural identity and pride, worn with a sense of reverence and tradition.
